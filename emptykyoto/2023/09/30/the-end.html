Saturday, September 30, 2023

The end of a Thrilling Year!

Well here we are folks! The end of a thrilling year, and the end of the publishing process for the Empty Kyoto book.

The idea for the book germinated in April of this year, fundraising started in July, the layout in August, printing September through November, shipping in December. And now we’re done!

Most all of you have received your books, although there are about a dozen orders still out there, notably shipments to Canada and shipments that went through New York City… (Photo courtesy of William Susman)

Now the book is out in the wild and I am seeing images like this one pop up on social media, which generates new orders as well. It’s a great feeling. Thank you.

I’m taking a small amount of time off, as everything in Japan closes down between ~December 30 and January 6. Our Osechi New Year’s meal arrived yesterday and is in the refrigerator. Our neighbor, a florist, is super busy preparing all kinds of New Year’s decorations and floral arrangements.

Thanks again for coming on this ride with me this year. There is more to come as I continue to promote the book and prepare for a gallery exhibit April 13-21 in Kyoto, as part of the Kyotographie International Photography festival. (More to come on this)

In January, I’ll be going back to California, followed by a trip to Seattle and Kansas to see my family, before returning to Kyoto in mid-March.

If you’re in the US and are interested in books or the 2024 calendars, I will be able to send them from California, saving time and money on the shipping. I have updated the website with the new pricing, effective until mid March.

I’m still experimenting with this newsletter and the supporters’ website, too. I started using Substack, which was okay, but I left because of their business practices. I moved to Ghost, which was also okay, but I wasn’t happy with their membership features. So now I’m using Memberful; here the membership features are good, but the newsletter/blogging features are lacking – actually they encourage you to host your newsletter and website elsewhere. I want more visual control of… well everything. All of these sites, by appealing to people with no web design experience, lock you into such a generic vanilla look, like this newsletter you’re reading now. It’s boring.

I’ve also moved the online purchasing component from Square to Stripe. I love Square for in-person transactions and have used them since they first launched in 2011. I have also used Stripe on and off for many years. Stripe is more web-focused and developer-centric, so a little harder to set up, but much better for the web in terms of localized user experience. I have customers all over the world and Stripe just feels better. I suppose something like Shopify might be the best online shopping solution, but I only have two products (the book and the calendar) and it seems like overkill to maintain a full blown online store. I already have a separare online photo gallery using SmugMug. It’s too much.

So things are still in flux. (Just like everything else in the world.)

Recently, I’ve been doing a number of things with Kyoto Journal, which if you don’t already know about you really should take a look at. A photo from Empty Kyoto is on the cover of the current issue, 105 and there is a feature about my book. I’ve been writing an article about Bagels & Sushi for their next issue, Cultural Fluidity, 106; and I’ve been shooting a lot of photos to accompany several articles in the following issue, Fire & Kyoto, 107, coming in the spring. There are very few magazines devoted to Japanese culture in English, and I’ve been reading the magazine for years. I used to visit the Kinokuniya book store in Little Tokyo, Los Angeles, to get the latest issues. So it’s a privilege to be able to work with them now.

I guess that’s about it. Best wishes for a safe and enjoyable holiday and the best of luck in 2024. We’ll need it.

– Daniel
