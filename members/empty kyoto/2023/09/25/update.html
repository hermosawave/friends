Monday, September 25, 2023

Empty Kyoto: 9/25 Update

As is the case with much of the northern hemisphere, it’s the last half of September and still the daily high temperature approaches 90° F. It is getting cooler though. I came to Japan for the first time in 1984, right around this time of the year. I remember the weather being very pleasant, not like this year. Things have changed!

Last week I received a print of 12 pages of the book, on the actual press, on the actual paper. That’s what’s at the top of this post. The images look great, but we decided I should improve one photo and replace one of the others.

I also asked about using slightly thicker paper. It’s fine the way it is but I’d just like a little more heft to the pages. This will, of course, cause printing and shipping to become more expensive; but if it’s not that much more I’m going to go for it. (Update: I went for it :-)

I’ve also been working on the cover this week. I can finish the cover most of the way, but it can’t be completed until we know how thick the book is; and the paper change will change the book thickness as well. Change one thing, and it changes many other things. I think most people have no idea how complicated manufacturing anything is.

2024 Calendar

I did a lot of prep work for the 2024 calendar last spring, anticipating a lack of time in which to prepare the calendar in the fall, which now seems to have been a good idea.

This year’s calendar theme is all Japan. I have about half the images set, but I’m trying to find a window for a day or two trip west of Kansai to acquire additional images for the calendar. Somewhere along the the Seto Inland Sea (Seto Naikai) area between Osaka and Hiroshima, but the weather and other commitments have conspired against me. Maybe this weekend.

Kyoto Journal Issue 105 is out!

If you don’t know about Kyoto Journal magazine, this is the latest issue of an English language periodical going back 35 years, showcasing the arts, culture and history of one of the world’s great cultural cities.

The latest - digital only - issue entitled, “The Geography of Myth”, features one of my photos from Empty Kyoto on the cover, an article about the book, and several other photos inside. You can find out more and purchase a pdf copy here:

www.kyotojournal.org/product/kyoto-journal-105-geography-of-myth/

All in all I’m feeling cautiously optimistic about things. The book is coming together. I have some viable packaging and shipping options. I made a shipping test to see how well, how much, and how fast my shipping concept will work.

Anyway thank you for coming on this journey with me – some of you for months, some for years. I hope all this effort will be worth it.

– Daniel
