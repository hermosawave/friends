Friday, October 20, 2023

Empty Kyoto: Moving Right Along

Hi folks, here’s the latest of what’s going on with the Empty Kyoto book.

Actually, things are kind of quiet right now. The eye of the storm, I guess.

I have signed off on the inside of the book, which means no more changes or approvals. The printer is “full speed ahead” on production.

I am waiting for proofs for the cover and dust jacket. I think I will receive them in the next few days. Update: I received the proofs yesterday. I asked for some changes to the color photo on the front cover (dust jacket). I think this will set things back a week, but maybe not. What takes the longest is the book itself - which I already approved - not the jacket.

The 2024 Calendars are here.

Some have been sent out, many are waiting to go along with the books. If you’d like one you can order at this link. Choose “Will Call” shipping and I will include it with your book.

I’ve acquired boxes, wrapping, and other packaging for both the books and calendars, and I think I have a configuration that will be efficient as well as visually appealing.

What I’ve mostly been doing the past few days is organizing the shipping itself. I set up business accounts with a few express shipping companies, and today I sent several test shipments to find out the following things:

How easy it is to ship a package internationally, can I do everything online or do I need to go someplace to fill out those multipart forms? Is the actual price they charge me is the same as their price list? How soon do they actually deliver? For the past 5 years or so I’ve been using JP Post (aka “the post office”) exclusively. But JP Post’s service really suffered during the pandemic, the lack of flights made things difficult. The US Postal Service has also made shipping to the US more difficult and expensive, by removing services that used to be available and by instituting more complex requirements. I sent a package last month by EMS (Express Mail) that JP Post says takes three days; it took seven days. Still figuring this out. But I have another month to get organized, so I have time to experiment before committing.

The Empty Kyoto digital book (PDF) is out!

If you’ve got one on order, you should have received a download link. If you didn’t please let me know. If would like a digital PDF version of the book, you can order here.

Anyway, that’s it for now.

Thanks again,

Daniel
