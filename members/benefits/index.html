HDR Images

Another thing I've been playing with is HDR photographs. HDR stands for High Dynamic Range, in other words brighter whites and blacker blacks.

I have been shooting photographs using HDR techniques for many years - this is a process where you take several photographs at different exposures and merge them together to get an image with a higher dynamic range. We compress the wider dynamic range into a narrower range used by the standard JPEG image format.

What I'm discussing here is my recent discovery that most Apple displays on MacBook Pros, iPads and iPhones support HDR images. It is now possible to deliver higher dynamic range images to the web browser.

This would be the equivalent of an audiophile remaster. Super high quality images but limited ways to use them. Instagram, Facebook or most other social networks do not support HDR images. Would this be interesting? Again, you can send a comment to any of the addresses in the lower right corner of this page.
